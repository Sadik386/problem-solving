Mymensingh Football History

From colonial-era tournaments to modern Premier League matches — Mymensingh has remained a heartbeat of Bangladeshi football.

Early Football Heritage

1898 — Birth of Organized Football

Mymensingh Mohammedan Sporting Club is believed to have been founded in 1898, making it one of the oldest football clubs in Bangladesh.

1914 — Historic Tournaments

The city hosted two of Bengal’s earliest football competitions: Surya Kanta Shield and Lila Devi Shield, establishing Mymensingh as a football powerhouse during the British era.

Post-Independence Era

After Bangladesh gained independence in 1971, Mymensingh continued producing national-level footballers. Players like Abdul Halim and Hassan Al-Mamun represented the country, carrying the district’s football legacy forward.

Rafiq Uddin Bhuiyan Stadium

Built in 1961, the stadium — also known as Mymensingh District Stadium — is the primary football venue of the region.

Capacity: 12,000
Sports: Football, Cricket, Cultural Events

Since 2016, it has regularly hosted Bangladesh Premier League matches and served as a home venue for multiple professional clubs.

Bangladesh Police FC & Modern Football

Bangladesh Police Football Club, founded in 1972, has used Mymensingh Stadium as a major home ground despite being based in Dhaka.

After winning the Bangladesh Championship League in 2019, the club entered the Premier League and achieved their best-ever 3rd place finish in 2022–23.

The stadium witnessed many important matches, including a 1–1 draw against Mohammedan SC on 19 October 2025.
